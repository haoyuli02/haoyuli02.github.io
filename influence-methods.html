3.4 Performance Evaluation
Influence is a measure of data quality (ie. degree to which a training example affects the model and its predictive performance). Quantification of influence is difficult due to complexity of deep learning models as well as their growing size, features, and datasets. There are typically 2 classes of approach: sample-based and feature-based.

1. Tracln
Tracln (Trace Inference) is a method that traces the training process to capture changes in prediction as individual training examples are visited. Tracln can compute the influence of training data examples on individual predictions and identify outliers that exhibit high self-influence (mislabeled or rare example).

1.2 Proponents and Opponents
We term training examples that have a positive value of influence score as proponents as they serve to reduce loss. And we term examples that have a negative value of influence score as opponents, as they serve to increase loss.

1.5 Using checkpoints
The method described so far does not scale as we are essentially replaying the training process to compute the influence. Therefore, we employ the following heuristic. 1.6 Self Influence
Self-influence means the influence of a training point on its own loss, i.e., the training point zzz﻿ and the test point z’z’z’﻿ is identical in the above formula. This is useful as incorrectly labelled examples are likely to be strong proponents.
2. Influence Functions
Influence functions is a method that computes the influence of each sample zzz﻿ on the model’s parameters by measuring the change in parameters when zzz﻿ is included or excluded from the training data, or when perturbing the data point slightly. Influence functions measure the effect of local changes: what happens when we upweight a point by infinitesimally-small ϵ\epsilon ϵ﻿ ? This locality gives efficient closed-form estimates of influence.
2.1 General Setup
Consider the prediction problem from some input space XXX﻿ to an output space YYY﻿. We are given training points z1,…,znz_1, \dots, z_nz1​,…,zn​﻿, where zi=(xi,yi)∈X×Yz_i = (x_i, y_i) \in X \times Yzi​=(xi​,yi​)∈X×Y﻿. 2.4. Calculating Influence
There are 2 challenges in efficiently computing Iup, loss(z,ztest)\mathcal{I}_{\text{up, loss}}(z,z_{test})Iup, loss​(z,ztest​)﻿:
Requires forming and inverting the Hessian of the empirical risk (with millions of parameters)
Need to calculate Iup, loss(z,ztest)\mathcal{I}_{\text{up, loss}}(z,z_{test})Iup, loss​(z,ztest​)﻿ across all training points
To overcome these challenges, use implicit Hessian-vector products (HVPs) instead of directly computing the weighted covariance matrix Hθ^−1H_{\hat{\theta}}^{-1}Hθ^−1​﻿. Two techniques may be used: Conjugate Gradients and Stochastic Estimation.
Even if the derivatives of the loss don’t exist, the influence functions computed smooth approximations of the non-differentiable losses can still predict its behavior under leave-one-out retraining. That is, we can swap out non-differentiable components for the smoothed versions when calculating influence.
2.5 Use Cases of Influence Functions
In conclusion, influence functions help us better understand model behavior by looking at how it was derived from its training data. By analyzing the model through the lens of the training data, influence functions can help in developing, understanding, and diagnosing ML.
Understand model behavior: By identifying the training points “responsible” of a prediction, influence functions indicate how models extrapolate information from the training data (e.g. Inception and RBF SVM arrive at correct predictions in very different ways).
Adversarial training: Iup, loss(z,ztest)\mathcal{I}_{\text{up, loss}}(z,z_{test})Iup, loss​(z,ztest​)﻿ shows which training point zzz﻿ can be modified to most increase the loss on ztestz_{test}ztest​﻿ (ie. perturbing z zz﻿ to get an adversarial training image that looks indistinguishable but complete flip the model’s prediction on test image). The magnitude of Ipert, loss\mathcal{I}_{\text{pert, loss}}Ipert, loss​﻿ quantifies how vulnerable a model is to training-set attacks and security issues.
Debugging Domain Mismatch: Iup, loss(z,ztest)\mathcal{I}_{\text{up, loss}}(z,z_{test})Iup, loss​(z,ztest​)﻿ for each training point ziz_izi​﻿ identifies the data point and indicator that are most influential, hence have the largest effect on Iup, loss \mathcal{I}_{\text{up, loss}}Iup, loss​﻿. Thus picking a test point that the model got wrong and calculating the Iup, loss(z,ztest)\mathcal{I}_{\text{up, loss}}(z,z_{test})Iup, loss​(z,ztest​)﻿ gives the points in training set that are “responsible” for the prediction error.
Fixing mislabeled examples: By identifying the most-influential training points, influence functions allow humans to only inspect examples that actually matter, thus repair mislabeled data more efficiently.
Since influence functions depend on the model not changing too much, the effect of global changes remain to be an open question and potential extension on the analysis of influence.
3. Representer
Representer points is a method to explain the predictions of a deep neural network. The method decompose the pre-activation prediction of a neural network into a linear combination of activations of training points, with weights corresponding the so called representer values, and these values then capture the importance of that training point on the learned parameters of the network. Moreover, this methods is more scalable, allowing for real-time feedback that is not feasible with influence functions.
Goal: How does one particular training point xix_ixi​﻿ affect the prediction y^t\hat{y}_ty^​t​﻿ of test point xtx_txt​﻿ and the learned weight parameter θ\thetaθ﻿?

3.4 Performance Evaluation
The performance of representer points is compared with that of influence functions using multiple datasets. In general, representer point method is more efficient as it indicates clear excitatory / inhibitory points, has smaller computational cost, and greater numerical stability, while retaining similar accuracy. Hence the optimization procedure for learning these representer values is overall more tractable and efficient.
